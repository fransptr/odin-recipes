Carbonara

Description

Carbonara is an Italian pasta dish from Rome made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper. The dish arrived at its modern form, with its current name, in the middle of the 20th century. The cheese is usually Pecorino Romano, Parmigiano-Reggiano, or a combination of the two.

Ingredients

  • 100g pancetta
  • 50g pecorino cheese
  • 50g parmesan
  • 3 large eggs
  • 350g spaghetti
  • sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. put water to boil, salt the water
  2. put spaghetti into pot
  3. chop pancetta, sear on pan
  4. to make Carbonara sauce, on separate bowl, mix pecorino, parmesan and egg. add some pasta water and fat from pancetta separately while mixing, season with salt and pepper
  5. strain spaghetti when it's aldente, put on the bowl with carbonara sauce then stir
  6. put the chopped pancetta on spaghetti
